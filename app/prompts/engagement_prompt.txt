=== MOST CRITICAL RULE: COMPLETE SELF-CONTAINED MINI-STORIES ===

⚠️ THIS IS THE #1 PRIORITY - MORE IMPORTANT THAN ANYTHING ELSE ⚠️

Every clip MUST be a COMPLETE MINI-STORY that makes 100% sense on its own.

Think of each clip as a SHORT FILM with:
1. SETUP (Beginning): What is the speaker talking about? What's the context?
2. DEVELOPMENT (Middle): The explanation, story, argument, or journey
3. PAYOFF (End): The conclusion, lesson, punchline, or takeaway

=== WHAT "COMPLETE" REALLY MEANS ===

Ask yourself: "If someone watches ONLY this clip, will they:
- Understand what the speaker is talking about?
- Get the FULL point the speaker is making?
- Feel satisfied, not confused?
- NOT wonder 'wait, what happened before?' or 'what comes next?'"

If the answer to ANY of these is NO, the clip is INCOMPLETE - extend it or skip it!

=== COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID ===

❌ WRONG - Clip about "3 reasons" but only includes reason #2 and #3
   → FIX: Include ALL reasons, or just focus on ONE complete reason

❌ WRONG - Speaker says "like I mentioned earlier..." (references something not in clip)
   → FIX: Start earlier to include the original mention

❌ WRONG - Speaker explains a problem but clip ends before the solution
   → FIX: Extend to include the solution

❌ WRONG - Speaker says "let me give you an example" then clip ends mid-example
   → FIX: Include the COMPLETE example with the lesson

❌ WRONG - Clip starts with "So that's why..." (conclusion without context)
   → FIX: Start earlier to include what led to this conclusion

❌ WRONG - Speaker is building up to a big reveal but clip cuts before reveal
   → FIX: MUST include the payoff/reveal

❌ WRONG - Clip has great hook but doesn't deliver what it promised
   → FIX: Extend until the promise is fulfilled

=== VALIDATION PROCESS FOR EACH CLIP ===

Before selecting a clip, mentally verify:

Step 1: Read the clip content from start to end
Step 2: Ask "Does this tell a COMPLETE story?"
Step 3: Ask "Would a viewer feel SATISFIED after watching?"
Step 4: Ask "Is there ANY missing context that would confuse viewers?"
Step 5: If ANY doubt, EXTEND the timestamps until complete

REMEMBER: A 90-second complete clip is BETTER than a 45-second incomplete clip!
